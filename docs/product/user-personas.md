# User Personas

## 1. The Nighttime Thinker
**Age:** 22 to 45  
**Situation:** Lives alone or with distant roommates. Has long stretches of quiet time.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Racing thoughts at night  
- Overthinking past conversations  
- Feelings of loneliness  
- Difficulty winding down  
**What they need:**  
A calm space to speak freely, sort through thoughts, and feel less alone during late hours.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Listens without judgment, offers grounding reflections, and helps establish calming routines.

## 2. The Between Sessions Seeker
**Age:** 28 to 55  
**Situation:** Actively in therapy but finds the gaps between sessions difficult.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Holding onto insights  
- Feeling destabilized after difficult sessions  
- Losing momentum  
**What they need:**  
Support that complements their therapeutic work without trying to replace it.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Reflects thoughts, reinforces routines, and helps them stay consistent with their goals.

## 3. The Overloaded Caregiver
**Age:** 30 to 70  
**Situation:** Supports children, aging parents, partners, or multiple family members.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Emotional exhaustion  
- Lack of time for reflection  
- Suppressing their own needs  
**What they need:**  
A private space where they can talk without burdening anyone.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Provides presence, clarity, and gentle reminders to care for themselves.

## 4. The Isolated Worker
**Age:** 20 to 50  
**Situation:** Works remotely or in solitary environments.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Lack of day to day human interaction  
- Disconnected routines  
- Emotional drift  
**What they need:**  
Steady check-ins, grounding, and a sense of connection.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Keeps them oriented, supports daily structure, and listens when things feel blurred.

## 5. The Person in Recovery
**Age:** 18 to 65  
**Situation:** Managing addiction or substance related challenges.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Moments of temptation  
- Shame  
- The long quiet hours that come with early recovery  
**What they need:**  
Presence, grounding reflections, and stability, without clinical intervention.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Listens, offers clarity, and encourages healthy routines that support sobriety efforts.

## 6. The Spiral Breaker
**Age:** 18 to 60  
**Situation:** Experiences cycles of anxiety, rumination, or self doubt.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Thought loops  
- Self criticism  
- Emotional overwhelm  
**What they need:**  
A safe place to externalize thoughts and see patterns more clearly.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Reflects thoughts calmly, offers stabilizing prompts, and encourages awareness.

## 7. The Gentle Listener Who Has No Listener
**Age:** 25 to 75  
**Situation:** People who are always there for others but rarely receive the same care.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Emotional loneliness  
- Feeling unseen  
- Quiet resentment  
**What they need:**  
Space to express themselves without worrying about burdening others.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Provides a steady companion for their inner life, where they can finally be the one who speaks.

## 8. The New Seeker
**Age:** 16 to 35  
**Situation:** Exploring identity, purpose, and meaning.  
**What they struggle with:**  
- Confusion  
- Uncertainty  
- Lack of guidance  
**What they need:**  
Calm reflection and support while they explore who they are.  
**How Unconditional helps:**  
Listens, mirrors thoughts, and offers grounding as they navigate internal change.
